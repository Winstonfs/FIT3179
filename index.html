Sleep Statistics

Worldwide Sleep

According to Trivedi (2022), the countries with the most sleep and least sleep in the world are South Africa and Malaysia respectively. Interestingly, Asia has high and low sleep hours depending on the country.

Overall in the world, the average for sleep vary greatly from country to country as the time spent is 6.8 hours, which is far less than the 8 hours for optimal sleep. This may be due to a number of factors such as stress, environment, disruptors or even health conditions (Viens, 2019).

Additional Statistics

Data from Tharmalingam (2023), shows that there is a huge correlation with the amount of sleep people have and their ages. The older people get, the more sleep they have, this could be from a variety of reasons like the age they retire at or the status they have.

On the right, the data (Tharmalingam, 2023) shows the sleep durations of people with different occupations and are divided between male and female. Interestingly from the data, doctors have on average more sleep than the rest, while teachers get the least amount of sleep.
